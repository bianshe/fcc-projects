Brief Biography:

  • Mirzakhani was born on 12 May 1977 in Tehran, Iran.
  • In 1994, Mirzakhani achieved the gold medal level in the International Mathematical Olympiad in Hong Kong, scoring 41 out of 42 points. She was the first female Iranian student to do so.
  • In 1995, she became the first Iranian student to achieve a perfect score and to win two gold medals in the International Mathematical Olympiad held in Canada.
  • In 1999, she obtained a Bachelor of Science in mathematics from the Sharif University of Technology. During her time there, she received recognition from the American Mathematical Society for her work in developing a proof for Shor's Algorithm.
  • In 2004, she earned a PhD from Harvard University, where she worked under the supervision of the Fields Medalist Curtis T. McMullen.
  • In 2009, she became a professor at Stanford University.
  • In 2013, Mirzakhani was diagnosed with breast cancer.
  • In 2014, with Alex Eskin and with input from Amir Mohammadi, Mirzakhani proved that complex geodesics and their closures in moduli space are surprisingly regular, rather than irregular or fractal.
  • In 2014, Mirzakhani was awarded the Fields Medal for "her outstanding contributions to the dynamics and geometry of Riemann surfaces and their moduli spaces". The award was made in Seoul at the International Congress of Mathematicians on 13 August.
  • In 2016, Maryam Mirzakhani was made a member of the National Academy of Sciences, making her the first Iranian woman to be officially accepted as a member of the academy.
  • She died on 14 July 2017 at the age of 40 at Stanford Hospital in Stanford, California.
